Understanding Skin Ageing

Published July 2026 · 10 min read

✏️ Research notes #002

Why Does Our Skin Age?

Ageing is a completely natural process. As we get older, our skin gradually produces less collagen and elastin—the proteins responsible for keeping skin firm, smooth and elastic. While this process cannot be stopped entirely, understanding why it happens allows us to make better choices that help our skin age as healthily as possible.

What Happens As We Age?

  • Collagen production gradually declines.
  • Skin becomes thinner and less elastic.
  • Fine lines slowly become more noticeable.
  • Wrinkles may deepen over time.
  • The skin may feel drier.
  • Age spots and uneven skin tone become more common.

What Speeds Up Skin Ageing?

  • Excessive sun exposure.
  • Smoking.
  • Poor nutrition.
  • Chronic stress.
  • Lack of sleep.
  • Environmental pollution.
  • Genetics.

Can Skin Ageing Be Slowed?

Although wrinkles are a normal part of ageing, healthy daily habits can help reduce premature skin ageing and improve the overall appearance of the skin.

  • Wear sunscreen every day.
  • Stay well hydrated.
  • Avoid smoking.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in antioxidants.
  • Get enough sleep.
  • Moisturise consistently.

Ingredients Worth Looking For

Many skincare products make bold promises, but a handful of ingredients have been studied for their ability to improve the appearance of ageing skin when used consistently.

  • Retinoids (Vitamin A).
  • Vitamin C.
  • Niacinamide.
  • Peptides.
  • Hyaluronic Acid.
  • Ceramides.

Results vary from person to person, and consistency is usually more important than using many products at once.

Why Are Peptides So Popular?

Peptides are small chains of amino acids that help support many of the skin's natural functions. Some peptide ingredients have been studied for their ability to improve the appearance of fine lines, wrinkles and skin firmness by supporting the skin's natural collagen renewal processes.

While no cream can stop ageing, peptide-based skincare has become an area of growing scientific interest in cosmetic dermatology.

When Should You See a Healthcare Professional?

Rapid skin changes, unusual growths, sores that do not heal, bleeding lesions, or sudden changes in moles should always be assessed by a qualified healthcare professional or dermatologist.

Key Takeaways

  • Skin ageing is a natural biological process.
  • Daily sunscreen remains one of the best anti-ageing habits.
  • Healthy lifestyle choices support healthier-looking skin.
  • Evidence-based ingredients such as retinoids, peptides and vitamin C may improve the appearance of ageing skin over time.
  • Consistency and patience are essential.
