What Causes Hyperpigmentation?

Published July 2026 · 8 min read

What is Hyperpigmentation?

Hyperpigmentation is a common skin condition where certain areas of the skin become darker than the surrounding skin due to an increase in melanin, the pigment responsible for skin colour. It is not a disease itself but rather a response to factors such as inflammation, sun exposure, hormonal changes, or skin injury.

What Causes Hyperpigmentation?

  • Acne and pimples
  • Sun exposure (UV damage)
  • Hormonal changes such as pregnancy
  • Skin injuries and burns
  • Certain medications
  • Inflammatory skin conditions

Can It Be Prevented?

  • Wear sunscreen every day.
  • Avoid picking at pimples.
  • Treat acne early.
  • Protect healing wounds from sunlight.
  • Follow a consistent skincare routine.

Treatment Options

  • Niacinamide
  • Vitamin C
  • Azelaic Acid
  • Retinoids
  • Chemical peels
  • Prescription creams when recommended by a healthcare professional.

When Should You See a Healthcare Professional?

If pigmentation develops suddenly, changes rapidly, is associated with pain or bleeding, or does not improve after several months of appropriate skincare, it is advisable to seek medical assessment to exclude other skin conditions.

Key Takeaways

  • Hyperpigmentation is common.
  • It is usually harmless.
  • Daily sunscreen is one of the most important preventive measures.
  • Most cases improve gradually with consistent treatment and patience.
